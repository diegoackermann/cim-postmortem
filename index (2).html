Context

POC Scope & Objectives

02
🎯

Objective

Validate AllUnite's WiFi sensor methodology for measuring OOH audience contacts across Belgian inventory, as a prerequisite to a full national rollout contract.

📡

Sensor Deployment

WiFi sensors installed at roadside billboards and transit shelters across 4 cities. Locations defined jointly via an inventory list agreed with CIM.

🏢

Media Owners

JCDecaux (largest), Bauer Media Outdoor, and Belgian Posters - all three agreed to host sensors during the POC period.

📶

Telco Data

Originally planned with Proximus (Belgium's largest telco). After Proximus withdrawal, replaced by Orange Belgium via partner Cropland, providing population movement data.

📊

Deliverables

Contact metrics per frame, demographic breakdowns, a validated methodology document, and a final presentation to CIM's Technical and Strategic Committees.

💶

Commercial Terms

€5,000 flat fee paid by CIM to AllUnite. A successful POC was intended to lead to a full multi-year national rollout contract covering 900+ sensor locations.

CIM flags that Docks Mall (transit/indoor) sensor data returns contact figures approximately 10x higher than reference benchmarks. Diego presented AllUnite's solution and full-rollout plans including a platform demo. Akshay presented the manual counting methodology and answered outstanding questions. Full Rollout Not Approved - Project Closes
CIM's Strategic Committee does not approve the full rollout contract. No formal reasons were provided. Based on the project history, contributing factors are likely: insufficient methodology confidence, unresolved data anomalies (Docks Mall), concerns about cost communication transparency, and supplier chain instability throughout the POC.

01
External FactorSep 2025 - First Critical Event

Proximus Withdraws from Project

⚠ CIM's Experience
  • Proximus was a named, budgeted partner in the agreed RFP at €20,500/year
  • The withdrawal disrupted CIM's internal planning and cost expectations
  • Proximus price increase to €43,000/year raised questions about the accuracy of original estimates
  • CIM's Technical Committee flagged telco data as essential for methodology validation
✦ AllUnite's Context
  • Proximus's decision to exit was unilateral and entirely outside AllUnite's control
  • AllUnite was first informed via partner Accurat - not directly by Proximus
  • AllUnite immediately escalated internally and began sourcing Orange as a replacement the same day
  • Accurat confirmed the situation affected all parties working on Belgium telco data - not unique to AllUnite
02
External FactorOct 7, 2025 - 7-Week Delay

CIM Requires Full Rollout Pricing Before POC Approval

⚠ CIM's Experience
  • CIM's Strategic Committee needed full rollout contract pricing from Orange before approving even the POC data use
  • Three POC options (€6,750-€8,150 excl. VAT) were presented but insufficient without long-term cost visibility
  • Governance concern: committing to a methodology trial without knowing the full-contract cost trajectory
✦ AllUnite's Context
  • AllUnite immediately requested full rollout pricing from Orange and committed to delivering promptly
  • Accurat advised proactively sharing Proximus's new pricing to contextualise Orange's competitiveness
  • AllUnite shared the comparative figures with CIM as recommended
  • Resolution reached Dec 1 following the joint CIM-Orange-AllUnite call on Nov 28
03
Internal IssueOct 2025 - Transparency Concern

SIM Card Costs Not Proactively Disclosed

⚠ CIM's Experience
  • CIM learned about SIM card costs reactively - not from a proactive AllUnite communication
  • Combined with the Proximus pricing change, this led CIM to raise a formal transparency concern in Oct 2025
  • Created doubt about whether other undisclosed costs might emerge later in the project
✦ AllUnite's Context
  • AllUnite absorbed SIM card costs for the POC to protect CIM's budget commitment
  • The intent was to avoid burdening CIM - not to obscure information
  • AllUnite management engaged directly with CIM to address the concern and find a path forward
  • Lesson learned: cost transparency must be maintained proactively, regardless of who absorbs the cost
04
External FactorDec 2025 - Technical Compromise with Rollout Implications

Sensor Installation Restrictions - No Drilling Permitted

⚠ CIM's Experience
  • Sensors placed inside screen housings rather than externally, limiting signal range from ~60m to ~10m
  • Docks (transit) data returned figures ~10x higher than reference benchmarks - initially unexplained
  • When the causal link to installation constraints was explained, it raised questions about data reliability for the full rollout
  • Media owners confirmed the process was operationally difficult and costly - and stated 900-sensor scale would not be feasible under the same approach
✦ AllUnite's Context
  • Drilling is prohibited by Belgian legislation - not a decision AllUnite or JCDecaux could override
  • AllUnite's Ops team maintained constant communication with all three media owner teams to find the best compliant solution within the legal constraints
  • Media owners directed AllUnite to contact David Gordillo (JCDecaux) directly through CIM - AllUnite did not have independent access
  • AllUnite began exploring AI camera-based alternatives to resolve the installation challenge for the rollout - though this did not reach a final proposal stage within the POC timeline
05
Shared FactorThroughout Project - Operational Blocker

Media Owner Access Channelled Through CIM

⚠ CIM's Experience
  • CIM served as the primary coordinator between AllUnite and all three media owners throughout the project
  • This was consistent with CIM's governance structure - they maintained control of media owner relationships
  • CIM directed AllUnite to contact media owners directly only when appropriate (e.g. David Gordillo for installation)
✦ AllUnite's Context
  • AllUnite's operations team needed direct access to media owner installation teams to resolve technical issues quickly
  • Routing all media owner communication through CIM added response time to operational decisions
  • When AllUnite requested direct contact, CIM would typically provide it - but this was not systematic from the outset
  • Lesson: agree direct technical contacts with media owners at project start for operational efficiency
06
Internal IssueThroughout 2025-2026 - Continuity Risk

Team Changes During Live Project

⚠ CIM's Experience
  • Multiple AllUnite contacts were added or changed across the project duration
  • Created a perception of instability and unclear ownership from CIM's perspective
  • New contacts joining mid-project sometimes required CIM to re-explain history or context
✦ AllUnite's Context
  • Team additions reflected genuine growth and specialisation: Kristina (comms cover), Rafe (telco), Artem (data), Valentina and Akshay (PM and delivery)
  • Each addition was intended to improve delivery capacity and response quality for CIM
  • Lesson: new team members must be formally introduced with a clear continuity brief so CIM never has to re-explain context
07
Shared FactorFeb-Jun 2026 - Final Deciding Factor

Methodology Documentation Delivered Late

⚠ CIM's Experience
  • CIM's Feb 6 email clarified they were evaluating the entire workflow - not only final numbers
  • Full methodology documentation was requested formally but delivered late in the process (May 28)
  • By the final committee review, insufficient process confidence had been built throughout the project
  • Committee did not approve the full rollout
✦ AllUnite's Context
  • AllUnite delivered all contracted POC data and metrics as agreed
  • Full methodology documentation was prepared and submitted to both committees by May 28
  • The full scope of documentation required was not formally defined until CIM's February 2026 email
  • Lesson: methodology docs should be a live shared artefact updated throughout - not a final-stage deliverable

Balanced Review

What We Did Well & Where We Can Improve

05

✓ What Went Well

  • Delivered 100% of contracted POC data and outputs within scope
  • Rapidly identified and onboarded Orange as a Proximus replacement within weeks of the withdrawal
  • AllUnite absorbed SIM card costs to protect CIM's budget commitment
  • Ops team maintained constant communication with all three media owners throughout installation challenges
  • Proactively organised the Nov 28 CIM-Orange meeting to rebuild confidence in the telco transition
  • 87,640+ sensor hours of data collected and all technical deliverables submitted on time

↑ Areas for Improvement

  • Cost changes must be disclosed proactively - regardless of who absorbs them
  • Third-party dependencies (e.g. Proximus) needed contingency planning and earlier risk disclosure
  • Installation constraints and their data quality impact should have been pre-flagged with a written analysis
  • Methodology documentation should be a live shared document - not delivered at the final stage
  • Team additions required structured client communication to maintain continuity of context
  • Direct operational contacts with media owners should be agreed at project start, not arranged reactively

Next Steps

Recommended Actions

06
▲ High Priority 4

Map Third-Party Risk Early

  • List all suppliers before project starts
  • Agree a backup option for each critical dependency
  • Include risk clause in contract

Always Flag Cost Changes

  • Inform the client of any cost change immediately
  • Even if AllUnite absorbs the cost
  • Transparency builds trust, silence erodes it

Share Methodology as You Go

  • Keep a shared doc updated throughout
  • Never leave it to the final stage
  • Client should see progress, not just a final report

Flag Installation Issues Immediately

  • If constraints affect data quality, say so in writing
  • Explain what changed and what the impact is
  • Propose solutions proactively
▶ Medium Priority 3

Understand Who Decides

  • Map the client's decision-making structure at kickoff
  • Brief each committee level regularly
  • Don't rely on one contact to cascade information

Manage Team Changes Formally

  • Introduce every new team member to the client
  • Give them full project context before first contact
  • Client should never have to re-explain history

Get Direct Operational Contacts

  • Agree direct contacts with media owners at kickoff
  • Don't route all operational decisions through the client
  • Faster response = fewer delays and less friction
▼ Low Priority 1

Include Rollout Pricing in Proposals

  • Show indicative full-contract cost ranges upfront
  • Committees need cost trajectory to approve even a POC
  • Prevents governance blockers mid-project
